My name is Reed, and I am a web developer in Washington DC. I've been specializing in front-end code since the turn of the century, working with small businesses, government agencies, digital marketing firms, and institutes of higher education.

I am passionate about social issues regarding gender, sexuality, and reproductive rights. I am a feminist, a night-owl, a bike-and-bus commuter, and a vegan baker. I drink coffee while I code, I knit socks based on my fandoms, and I sing in a team-based social karaoke league.

Lifelong learning leads to a better understanding of changing needs.

Accessibility is an important component in emerging tech, especially in web development. Mobile first, responsive design, and progressive enhancement are just a few of my favorite strategies. I read blogs from industry professionals on front-end design and development, including HTML, CSS, responsive web design, accessibility, and usability. I've attended (and/or will again attend!) serveral conferences, including Design4Drupal, Drupal GovCon, CSS Dev Conf, and Lesbians Who Tech.

I am in the process of applying to graduate school, but I am currently finishing the requirements for a Professional Certificate in Information Accessibility & Design from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. I also hold undergraduate degrees in Digital Media & Web Technology from the University of Maryland, University College and Internet Operations and Development from the Community College of Philadelphia.
